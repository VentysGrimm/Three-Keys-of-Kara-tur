# 00 - Act I Start Here

## Act Title

**Act I — The Temple Lie**

## Campaign

**Three Keys of Kara-Tur**

## Level Range

Levels 1–3.

## Play Mode

Solo player character with rotating NPC companions.

## Tone

Sacred coming-of-age, hidden identity drama, divine horror, romantic tension, and betrayal.

## Act Theme

> The home that raised you is also the first lie.

## Premise

The player character begins as a young monk raised in the Temple of the Dawn Chain, a mountain monastery in western Kara-Tur. The temple publicly teaches discipline, compassion, and divine service. Students are told they train to protect the world from ancient disasters.

This is true.

It is also incomplete.

The temple's secret purpose is to train living sutras: mortals capable of renewing divine seals through body, breath, divine vow, and sacrifice. These seals hold back three world-ending disasters:

1. The Tarrasque, watched by the Father of Monsters.
2. The Slivers, hunted and contained by Storjeger, the Great Hunter.
3. The Aboleth, drowned beneath the venomous shadow of Zehir.

The player begins on the day of the Three Bow Ceremony, when young monks are forced to choose which divine seal they will serve.

## Core Truth

Grandmaster Iwamoto is secretly Atsumori Nine-Tails, an immortal kitsune cursed with endless life. He helped create the living sutra rites long ago. Now, after centuries of grief, he wants to die.

His plan is not simple destruction. He wants to awaken enough apocalyptic force to break his immortality curse. To do that, he needs the player and their companions to become strong, frightened, devoted, suspicious, and desperate enough to seek the three great seals.

Act I is his opening move.

## Running Solo Play

The player makes the major choices. NPCs advise, argue, flirt, protect, or challenge, but they do not decide the campaign direction.

NPCs help in combat but do not solve the story. Yuko may notice tracks, Joka may identify poison, and Jon Soo may protect the player, but the key clue, final decision, and emotional turning point should belong to the player.

Relationships are part of the adventure. Romance, rivalry, jealousy, trust, betrayal, and found-family loyalty are campaign engines.

Secrets should unfold slowly. Almost every important NPC is pretending to be something they are not. Foreshadow first.
