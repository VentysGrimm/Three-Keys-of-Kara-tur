# 06 - Act I Secrets, Rewards, and Failure States

## Act I GM Secrets

### Secret 1: Iwamoto Arranged the Beast Attack

The Seal-Warped Beasts were not random. Iwamoto allowed corrupted creatures onto temple grounds to awaken fear and purpose in the chosen students.

He did not intend for the player to die.

He was willing to risk it.

### Secret 2: The Minor Seal Was Deliberately Weakened

The Shrine of the Buried Bell failed because Iwamoto's hidden agents damaged the rite. The Bell-Eater Grub was drawn by the broken resonance.

### Secret 3: The Fox Was Once a Hero

Atsumori Nine-Tails helped create the living sutra rites. He was not always the villain. His betrayal came later, after immortality became a curse.

### Secret 4: Iwamoto Wants the Player to Discover the Truth

He is not hiding the mystery perfectly. He is guiding the player through layers of revelation.

He wants the player strong enough to reach the great seals.

He wants them suspicious enough to question him.

He wants them angry enough to fight him.

He wants them compassionate enough to understand why he wants to die.

### Secret 5: Seigen Is Not Loyal to Iwamoto

Master Seigen serves his own agenda. He may oppose Iwamoto, but that does not make him safe.

He wants Joka protected. He wants Zehir's mysteries preserved. He wants leverage over everyone.

### Secret 6: Jon Soo's Oath May Become a Threat

Jon Soo's Decree of Genocide can turn him from protector into danger. His future arc should ask whether vengeance can be restrained before it becomes another apocalypse.

### Secret 7: Yuko's Feelings Complicate Her Mission

Yuko was trained to seduce, infiltrate, and manipulate. Her feelings for the player may begin as part of a role, but they can become real.

This terrifies her.

### Secret 8: Joka May Become Rival, Lover, or Betrayer

Joka is not loyal to the player by default. She respects strength, honesty, and survival. If the player earns her trust, she may become one of their fiercest defenders.

If betrayed, she will remember forever.

## Treasure and Rewards

### Divine Mark

The player receives a minor divine mark based on their chosen altar.

### First Bell Fragment

A bronze shard from the Shrine of the Buried Bell. Required for later sealing rituals.

### Fox-Marked Scroll Case

Contains the old map, damaged founding sutra, and altered copy in Iwamoto's hand.

### NPC Bonds

By the end of Act I, the player should have meaningful emotional ties with:

- Yuko
- Joka
- Jon Soo
- Master Ye Sol
- Master Seigen
- Grandmaster Iwamoto

## Failure States

Failure should complicate the story, not stop it.

### If the Player Falls During the Beast Attack

They awaken in the infirmary. Iwamoto saved them and said:

> "Not yet."

A younger student may have died, increasing guilt and stakes.

### If the Player Fails at Ash Millet

More villagers suffer nightmares. The Bell-Eater Grub gains extra HP from feeding.

### If the Player Fails the Archive Challenge

They still obtain the map, but lose something:

- The Bell Fragment
- Seigen's trust
- Yuko's safety
- Joka's restraint
- Jon Soo's secrecy
- Ye Sol's protection

### If the Player Refuses to Leave the Temple

Master Ye Sol urges them to investigate the three marked sites.

> "You can confront a locked door forever, or find the key before the lock knows you are looking."

## Transition to Act II

Act II begins when the player chooses the first major destination.

### The Bone-Maw Mountains

Best for Tarrasque lore, giant ruins, endurance trials, ancient bones, and the Father of Monsters.

### The Moon-Hunted Forest

Best for Sliver lore, survival horror, pursuit, adaptive enemies, Yuko's secrets, and Storjeger's vow.

### The Black Glass Coast

Best for Aboleth lore, poison, drowned cities, Zehir's mysteries, Joka's arc, and Seigen's hidden agenda.

No matter which path the player chooses, Grandmaster Iwamoto is already ahead of them.

Not physically.

Historically.

Every road they take was prepared by someone who wanted them to believe they were choosing freely.
