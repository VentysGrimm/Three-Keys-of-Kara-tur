# 08 - Location: Ash Millet

## Foundry Journal Target

`Act I / Locations / Village of Ash Millet`

## Location Role

Ash Millet is the first place where the player sees that temple mysteries harm ordinary people. The village should contrast sharply with the Temple of the Dawn Chain: poor, practical, tired, and suspicious of holy explanations.

## Read-Aloud: Arrival

The road drops out of cedar forest into steep millet terraces cut into the mountainside.

Ash Millet is smaller than the temple maps suggested. Its houses lean against stone walls blackened by old cooking smoke. Prayer cords hang over doorways, but most are frayed. Ancestor tablets have been stacked outside one home where the wall cracked during the night.

No one rushes to greet you.

A goat bleats once, then goes silent.

From somewhere beneath the fields comes a faint bronze sound.

Not a bell.

A heartbeat pretending to be one.

## GM Summary

Ash Millet is suffering because the nearby Shrine of the Buried Bell has failed. The failed shrine once helped quiet Tarrasque-linked dreams and tremors in the region. Now nightmares, livestock disappearances, and earth tremors have returned.

The villagers are not ignorant. They know the temple has power, and they know power rarely arrives without cost.

## Public Problems

- Livestock vanish at night.
- Wells taste of iron.
- Tremors crack grain jars and ancestor tablets.
- Farmers dream of a mountain breathing beneath them.
- Children hear bells below the fields.
- Villagers are exhausted from nightmares.

## Hidden Cause

The Shrine of the Buried Bell was deliberately weakened. The Bell-Eater Grub was drawn to the resonance and began feeding on bronze, bells, livestock, and fear.

Grandmaster Iwamoto did not personally attack the village, but his manipulation led to this suffering.

## Key NPCs

### Elder Hana

Practical, exhausted, and wary of temple authority.

**Read-Aloud Introduction:**

An older woman steps from the largest house, leaning on a hoe as if it is both tool and weapon. Her hair is bound with a strip of gray cloth. Her eyes move from your robes to your hands, then to the weapons and travel dust of your companions.

"You monks always come after the shaking starts," she says. "Never before."

**Use:** moral pressure, village authority, local history.

### Toma, Goat-Herder Child

A frightened child who saw the creature's tracks.

**Line:** "It had too many legs, but it rang like festival bronze."

**Use:** route to the shrine, emotional stakes, evidence of livestock attacks.

### Old Man Ren

Keeper of forbidden local stories.

**Line:** "My grandfather said the bell was not built to wake gods. It was built to keep one sleeping."

**Use:** old lore, shrine context, foreshadowing the Tarrasque.

## Investigation Structure

The player needs enough clues to reach the Shrine of the Buried Bell. Do not let the investigation stall.

### Core Clues

- Tracks lead toward the shrine.
- The bell sound comes from underground.
- The village wells resonate with the shrine.
- Old Man Ren knows the bell was meant to keep something asleep.
- A child saw a black prayer strip near the shrine path.

### Fallback Rule

After two failed checks, Yuko finds a partial track, Joka notices bronze residue, or Jon Soo hears the underground bell clearly enough to point the way.

The player should still decide whether to go.

## NPC Companion Use

### Yuko

Yuko comforts children and studies tracks. She should be warm here.

Possible line:

> "Fear leaves footprints too. These people have been walking in circles around theirs."

### Joka

Joka tastes the well water and identifies that fear and resonance are interacting with the village.

Possible line:

> "This is not poison. Not exactly. Poison enters the blood. This enters sleep."

### Jon Soo

Jon Soo drinks with farmers and quietly asks who vanished first.

Possible line:

> "Villages know things temples forget to write down. Listen to the people who have to repair the walls after holy men explain the cracks."

## Moral Question

A villager should eventually ask:

> "Are you here to save us, or to make us feel saved?"

This echoes Iwamoto's manipulation and should make the player question temple authority.

## Secrets to Reveal

1. The shrine has not been maintained honestly.
2. Someone tied temple prayer material near the shrine.
3. The village remembers versions of history the temple does not teach.
4. The failed seal is connected to sleeping earth, bells, and the Tarrasque.
5. The temple's negligence, or deliberate manipulation, has consequences for common people.
