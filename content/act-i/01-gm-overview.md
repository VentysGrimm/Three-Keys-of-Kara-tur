# 01 - Act I GM Overview

## The Temple of the Dawn Chain

The Temple of the Dawn Chain is a mountain monastery in western Kara-Tur. It is built into cliffs, cedar forests, old stone stairs, and mist-covered courtyards.

### Public Purpose

To train monks in discipline, compassion, and divine service.

### Hidden Purpose

To train living sutras capable of renewing divine seals through body, breath, vow, and sacrifice.

## Important Locations

### Hall of Lamps

The ceremonial heart of the temple. Contains the three sealed altars used in the Three Bow Ceremony.

### Moon Gate Courtyard

Training yard where students spar. Joka often practices here after dark.

### Grandmaster's Tea Room

A simple room with a low table, old kettle, and mountain view. Beneath one floor mat is a faint fox-shaped burn mark.

### Western Archive

A cliffside library containing censored histories, copied sutras, forbidden maps, and sealed medical records.

### Medicine Hall

Master Seigen's domain. Smells of bitter roots, incense, venom, and boiled rainwater.

### Sutra Caves

Meditation caves beneath the temple. Students are told these are the deepest chambers.

### The Sealed Lower Stair

An old stairway below the Sutra Caves. Students are forbidden to enter. The fourth map mark points deeper than this stair.

## The Three Divine Vows

During Session 1, the player chooses one of three altars. Yuko and Joka also choose publicly. Jon Soo does not publicly choose.

The player's choice should matter spiritually and narratively, but it does not need to restrict class choice.

### Altar of the Bone Cradle

- **Symbol:** A bone cradle filled with flowers.
- **Vision:** A mountain breathing like a sleeping beast.
- **God:** The Father of Monsters.
- **Seal:** The Tarrasque.
- **Omen:** The player hears a heartbeat beneath stone.
- **Minor Mark:** Once per long rest, when the player is knocked prone or moved against their will, they may immediately reduce the forced movement by 10 feet or stand without spending movement.
- **Burden:** Animals sometimes flinch from the player. Large stone structures seem to breathe when the player is alone.

### Altar of the Silver Spear

- **Symbol:** A silver spear beneath a red moon.
- **Vision:** A forest running away from the hunter.
- **God:** Storjeger, the Great Hunter.
- **Seal:** The Slivers.
- **Omen:** The player hears distant horns and many feet moving as one.
- **Minor Mark:** Once per long rest, the player may add 1d4 to an initiative roll or Wisdom (Survival) check.
- **Burden:** Reflections of the player sometimes show them being watched by something with too many eyes.

### Altar of the Black Serpent

- **Symbol:** A black serpent biting its own shadow.
- **Vision:** An ocean whispering names backward.
- **God:** Zehir.
- **Seal:** The Aboleth.
- **Omen:** The player briefly forgets their own name, then remembers it in a voice that is not theirs.
- **Minor Mark:** Once per long rest, the player may gain advantage on a saving throw against poison or fear.
- **Burden:** Still water sometimes ripples toward the player, even with no wind.

## Recurring Symbols

- **Bells:** sleep, warning, sealing, and awakening.
- **Chains:** duty and sacrifice.
- **Fox Marks:** hidden, erased, damaged, or burned truths.
- **Breath:** living sutra training and eventual sacrificial rites.
- **Poison:** truth entering the body.
- **Moonlight:** Yuko, hunting, attraction, and hidden forms.
