# 10 - Location: Western Archive

## Foundry Journal Target

`Act I / Locations / Western Archive`

## Location Role

The Western Archive is the Act I truth chamber. It reveals that temple history has been rewritten, introduces the fox-marked scroll case, and turns investigation into flight when the Masked Temple Guardians arrive.

It should feel quiet, tall, dusty, fragile, and dangerous because knowledge itself is being hunted.

## Read-Aloud: Arrival

The western archive is built into the cliffside, its wooden beams dark with age and mountain damp.

Inside, the air smells of cedar dust, lamp oil, and medicinal smoke. Scroll shelves climb three stories into shadow. Narrow bridges connect the upper stacks. Thousands of prayer tablets hang from red cords, clicking softly though there is no wind.

Master Seigen stands beside a reading table covered in scrolls.

His hands are folded inside his sleeves. A single cup of tea waits untouched beside him.

"You saw something you should not have," he says.

His eyes remain human.

Mostly.

"The question is whether you understood it."

## GM Summary

The archive stores official temple histories, copied sutras, censored founding documents, medical records, shrine maps, and forbidden scrolls. Some records are original. Some are careful copies. Some have been altered by Iwamoto.

The Western Archive sequence has three purposes:

1. Let Seigen reveal partial truths.
2. Give the player evidence of the erased fourth figure.
3. Force the party into a skill challenge after the Masked Temple Guardians attack.

## Archive Features

- Three-story scroll stacks.
- Narrow upper bridges.
- Hanging prayer tablets that click softly.
- Rolling ladders.
- Locked scroll cabinets.
- Cliffside windows.
- Hidden venom-ink notes.
- Warded record cases.
- A concealed fox-marked scroll case.

## What Seigen Reveals

Seigen should reveal enough to push the campaign forward, but not everything.

### Core Revelations

1. Temple histories have been rewritten.
2. The three seals were not maintained by gods alone.
3. A fourth figure once stood beside the divine seal-keepers.
4. That figure has been removed from nearly every record.
5. Grandmaster Iwamoto's hand appears on recent alterations.
6. Some records are not in the archive, but beneath the temple.

### Seigen's Motives

Choose or combine:

- He wants leverage over Iwamoto.
- He wants to protect Joka from being used as a sacrifice.
- He believes Zehir's seal is weakening faster than the others.
- He wants to test whether the player is useful.
- He suspects the player may expose him and wants control of the situation.

### Seigen Line

> "Do not mistake my secrets for his. Mine are smaller, cleaner, and better labeled."

## Masked Temple Guardians Attack

### Read-Aloud

The prayer tablets stop clicking.

For a moment, the whole archive holds its breath.

Then white masks appear between the shelves.

No one shouts. No one announces judgment. The guardians simply step forward with hooked chains, sealed staves, and smoke-blackened prayer strips.

Master Seigen sighs.

"Earlier than I expected," he says.

### Guardian Truth

- Iwamoto arranged the attack as another test.
- Most guardians believe this is a sanctioned loyalty trial.
- They are not supposed to kill the party.
- They are supposed to frighten, separate, and measure them.
- Seigen suspected an interruption but did not know the exact timing.

## Guardian Traits

Use 4–6 guardians depending on party strength. Give each one a trait.

| Trait | Effect |
|---|---|
| Hooked chain | Can attempt a 10-foot grapple |
| Smoke pellet | Once creates light obscurement in a 10-foot area |
| Prayer seal | Once imposes disadvantage on a spell attack or concentration check |
| Staff fighter | +1 AC while wielding staff |
| Silent step | Advantage on Stealth checks |
| Venom needle | On hit, DC 11 Con save or lose reactions for 1 round |

## Skill Challenge: Escape the Archive

The party must get 5 successes before 3 failures.

The player rolls for major decisions. NPCs provide support, grant advantage, or absorb consequences.

### Suggested Checks

| Skill | Use |
|---|---|
| Acrobatics | Leap between shelves or swing from prayer cords |
| Athletics | Hold a collapsing archive door or shove a shelf into pursuers |
| History | Identify the oldest exit routes |
| Investigation | Find the hidden fox-marked scroll case |
| Religion | Recognize a false prayer seal |
| Stealth | Avoid temple guardians |
| Perception | Hear guardians moving above or below |
| Arcana | Disrupt a warded archive lock |
| Medicine | Identify Seigen's venom-lock mechanism |

### DCs

- DC 12 for straightforward actions.
- DC 14 for risky or clever actions.
- DC 16 for desperate actions under pressure.

### NPC Support

**Jon Soo: Drunken Diversion.** Cancel one failure or give advantage on Athletics, Acrobatics, or Deception.

**Yuko: Moonlit Path.** Give advantage on Stealth, Perception, or Survival.

**Joka: Poisoned Needle.** Remove one guardian complication or give advantage on Sleight of Hand, Medicine, or Stealth.

**Master Seigen: Serpent's Key.** Automatically reveal the fox-marked scroll case, but the player now owes Seigen a favor.

| 4 | Joka kills a guardian too quickly, escalating the scene |

| 6 | Seigen abandons the group temporarily to protect his own secrets |

## Fox-Marked Scroll Case

The scroll case is black lacquer with a barely visible fox mark under the dust.

Inside are:

1. An old map of Kara-Tur.
2. A damaged founding sutra.
3. A recent copy in Iwamoto's hand that removes all references to "the fourth."

## Secrets to Reveal

1. Iwamoto's hand appears on altered records.
2. The living sutra rites came from the erased fourth figure.
3. The fourth mark points beneath the Temple of the Dawn Chain.
4. Seigen is using the truth to gain leverage.
5. The guardian attack is a test, not an execution.
6. The truth is dangerous because Iwamoto wants the party to find it eventually.
