# 12 - Act I GM Quick Reference

## Act I Title

**Act I — The Temple Lie**

## Level Range

Levels 1–3.

## Core Theme

> The home that raised you is also the first lie.

| Moment | Level |
|---|---:|
| Campaign begins | 1 |
| After Seal-Warped Beast attack | 2 |

## Session 1: The Choosing of Chains

### Purpose

Introduce temple life, companion dynamics, divine vows, the first corrupted attack, and the prayer tag clue.

### Scene Flow

1. Dawn at the Temple
2. Morning Interactions
3. Three Bow Ceremony
4. Hall of Lamps visions
5. Seal-Warped Beast attack
6. Prayer Tag discovery
7. Aftermath

### Key Choice

The player chooses a divine vow:

| Vow | God | Seal | Minor Mark |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bone Cradle | Father of Monsters | Tarrasque | resist forced movement / stand from prone |
| Silver Spear | Storjeger | Slivers | add 1d4 to initiative or Survival |
| Black Serpent | Zehir | Aboleth | advantage vs poison or fear |

### Encounter

Seal-Warped Beast attack.

Use 3 corrupted beasts for solo PC + Jon Soo + Yuko + Joka.

Add one mutation per beast.

### Critical Clue

Prayer Tag in Iwamoto's Hand:

> "Begin with fear. Let them believe the world needs them."

### End

Player advances to level 2.

## Session 2: The First Dead Seal

### Purpose

Show the cost of failed seals on common people, reveal erased fox history, recover the First Bell Fragment.

### Scene Flow

1. Iwamoto's assignment
2. Road to Ash Millet
3. Village investigation
4. Shrine of the Buried Bell
5. Bell-Eater Grub boss fight
6. Return to Ash Millet

### Key Investigation Clues

- Villagers dream of a mountain breathing.
- Wells taste of iron.
- Livestock vanish toward the shrine path.
- Old Man Ren says the bell kept something sleeping.
- Fox-faced shrine statues were defaced.
- The mural includes a scratched-out fourth figure with traces of nine tails.

### Boss

Bell-Eater Grub.

- AC 14
- HP 60
- Bite +5, 1d8+3 piercing
- Spasm pushes nearby targets
- Bloodied scream frightens on failed DC 12 Wisdom save

### Reward

First Bell Fragment.

Once before the end of Act II, the player may ring it as an action. Hostile creatures within 15 feet make a DC 12 Wisdom save or cannot take reactions until the start of the player's next turn.

### End

Player advances to level 3.

## Session 3: The Grandmaster's Test

### Purpose

Test the meaning of sacrifice, reveal altered histories, introduce the old map, and launch Act II.

### Scene Flow

1. Return to temple
2. Private companion reactions
3. Iwamoto's question
4. Seigen's warning
5. Western Archive
6. Masked Temple Guardians attack
7. Archive escape skill challenge
8. Fox-Marked Scroll Case
9. Act I choice and Act II route

### Iwamoto's Question

> "Would you die to preserve the seal?"

Use answer to define Iwamoto's future interest in the player.

### Archive Escape

5 successes before 3 failures.

Recommended DCs:

- DC 12 straightforward
- DC 14 risky or clever
- DC 16 desperate

### Skill Options

- Acrobatics
- Athletics
- History
- Investigation
- Religion
- Stealth
- Perception
- Arcana
- Medicine

| NPC | Support |

| Yuko | advantage on Stealth / Perception / Survival |
| Joka | remove one guardian complication or advantage on Sleight of Hand / Medicine / Stealth |

Fox-Marked Scroll Case containing:

1. Old Map of Kara-Tur

## Failure Guidance

Failure should complicate, not stop.

| Failure | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Falls during beast attack | wakes in infirmary; Iwamoto says "Not yet" |
| Fails Ash Millet investigation | NPC companion finds fallback route to shrine |
| Loses Bell-Eater fight | village suffers more; party recovers later at cost |
| Fails archive challenge | escape succeeds, but item, trust, secrecy, or safety is lost |
| Confronts Iwamoto early | he redirects with partial truths and Act II hooks |
| Investigates fourth mark early | finds sealed stair but cannot open it yet |

## Act I Secrets

1. Iwamoto arranged the beast attack.
2. The Shrine of the Buried Bell was deliberately weakened.
3. The fox-marked fourth figure was once part of the founding history.
4. Iwamoto wants the player to uncover the lie eventually.
5. Seigen is not loyal to Iwamoto.
6. Jon Soo's oath may become dangerous.
7. Yuko's feelings complicate her hidden mission.
8. Joka can become rival, lover, defender, or betrayer.
