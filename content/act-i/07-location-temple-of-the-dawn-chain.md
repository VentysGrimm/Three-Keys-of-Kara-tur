# 07 - Location: Temple of the Dawn Chain

## Foundry Journal Target

`Act I / Locations / Temple of the Dawn Chain`

## Location Role

The Temple of the Dawn Chain is the Act I home base, training ground, spiritual prison, and first mystery site. It should feel safe before it feels suspicious.

The player was raised here. Every stone should carry memory. Every familiar bell should become more ominous as Act I progresses.

## Read-Aloud: First View

The Temple of the Dawn Chain clings to the mountain like a vow carved into stone.

Tiered roofs rise through cedar mist. Prayer flags snap between red pillars. Bronze bells hang beneath every eave, each one bound with a strip of white cloth and a thread of black chain.

Below the eastern cliffs, cloud covers the valley like an inland sea. Above, the old stair climbs toward the Hall of Lamps, where novices become servants of gods.

This is home.

That is what everyone has always told you.

## GM Summary

The temple publicly trains monks, healers, scribes, scouts, and divine servants. Its hidden purpose is to prepare living sutras: mortals capable of renewing the seals that hold back the three great disasters.

Grandmaster Iwamoto has shaped the temple for centuries. Some lies are old institutional habit. Some are recent alterations by his own hand.

## Public Truths

- The temple protects Kara-Tur from ancient disasters.
- Students train in body, breath, discipline, compassion, and divine service.
- The Three Bow Ceremony binds students to one of three divine seal traditions.
- The temple teaches that sacrifice is sacred.
- The Sutra Caves are said to be the deepest part of the monastery.

## Hidden Truths

- The temple is built over the hidden fourth seal.
- A nine-tailed fox symbol was removed from older temple art and records.
- Grandmaster Iwamoto is Atsumori Nine-Tails.
- The temple's living sutra rites were taught to mortals by the erased fourth figure.
- Iwamoto wants the player to discover the lie, but only after becoming useful.

## Key Areas

### Hall of Lamps

Ceremonial chamber containing the three lit altars of the Three Bow Ceremony.

Use for:

- Session 1 divine vow choice.
- First visions of the Father of Monsters, Storjeger, or Zehir.
- Early divine mark acquisition.

Foundry Scene Target: `Hall of Lamps`

### Moon Gate Courtyard

Open training court where students spar. Moonlight falls through a circular gate carved with old chain patterns.

Use for:

- Joka's challenge.
- Student rivalries.
- Later sparring scenes.
- Optional social or romantic beats.

Foundry Scene Target: `Moon Gate Courtyard`

### Grandmaster's Tea Room

A sparse room overlooking the eastern cloud sea. A low table, old kettle, and four worn floor cushions sit beside sliding screens.

A faint fox-shaped burn mark lies beneath one mat.

Use for:

- Iwamoto conversations.
- Moral tests.
- Tea ceremony scenes.
- Foreshadowing Atsumori.

Foundry Scene Target: `Grandmaster's Tea Room`

### Medicine Hall

Master Seigen's domain. Shelves hold herbs, venom jars, antidote powders, bone needles, and scrolls written in medicinal shorthand.

Use for:

- Seigen's warnings.
- Poison lore.
- Joka's training foreshadowing.
- Recovery after failure.

Foundry Scene Target: `Medicine Hall`

### Western Archive

Cliffside library containing censored histories, copied sutras, sealed medical records, forbidden maps, and altered founding scrolls.

Use for:

- Session 3 revelations.
- Masked Guardian attack.
- Archive escape skill challenge.

Foundry Scene Target: `Western Archive`

### Sutra Caves

Meditation caves below the temple. Students are told these are the monastery's deepest chambers.

Use for:

- Breath training.
- Forbidden dreams.
- Hints of the sealed lower stair.

### Sealed Lower Stair

A forbidden stair below the Sutra Caves. It bears four old symbols:

- Bone cradle
- Silver spear
- Black serpent
- Nine-tailed fox, burned black

This stair cannot be opened in Act I without knowledge from the three Act II destinations.

## Secrets to Reveal Slowly

1. The temple records have been edited repeatedly.
2. The Sutra Caves are not the deepest level.
3. The fourth symbol has been burned away in several locations.
4. Iwamoto's handwriting appears in altered sources.
5. Ye Sol suspects him but does not know the full truth.
6. Seigen knows more than he admits.
