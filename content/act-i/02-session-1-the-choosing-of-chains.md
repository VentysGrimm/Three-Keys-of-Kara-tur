# 02 - Session 1: The Choosing of Chains

## Session Purpose

Introduce the temple, the main NPC party, the divine vows, the first attack, and the first clue that Grandmaster Iwamoto is manipulating events.

## Opening Mood

The first half of the session should feel sacred and personal. The temple is home. The player should feel raised, known, and watched.

## Scene 1: Dawn at the Temple

### Read-Aloud

The first bell rings before sunrise.

Its note rolls through the cedar beams, through the cold stone, through your ribs. Beyond the eastern cliffs, clouds cover the valleys like a white sea. Prayer flags snap in the mountain wind.

Today, no one calls you a novice.

Today, you enter the Hall of Lamps.

Today, you choose the chain that will bind you to a god.

Master Ye Sol walks past, adjusting your stance with two fingers before you can object.

"Stand straight," she says. "Not because the gods care. Because I do."

Across the courtyard, Grandmaster Iwamoto smiles into his tea as though he has heard a joke no one else remembers.

### GM Prompts

Ask one or more:

- What temple chore did you always hate?
- Which teacher frightened you most as a child?
- When did Yuko first become important to you?
- What did Joka do that made you respect or resent her?
- What did Jon Soo once teach you that no master approved of?
- What secret of Master Seigen did you discover?

Use these answers later.

## Scene 2: Morning Interactions

Before the ceremony, let the player speak with the main NPCs.

### Yuko Yanagi

Yuko finds the player near the prayer flags.

> "Everyone is pretending not to be afraid. It is almost impressive."

If the player admits fear:

> "Good. Fear has better eyes than pride."

Possible romantic beat: Yuko fixes the player's collar or sash and lets the touch linger slightly too long.

### Jon Soo

Jon Soo is sitting where he is not supposed to sit, drinking from a flask that may or may not contain tea.

> "Advice from your older brother: when a god offers you destiny, check both sleeves for knives."

If asked which altar he will choose:

> "Gods and I have an arrangement. I avoid them, they disappoint me from a respectful distance."

This is a lie.

### Joka

Joka approaches the player with a challenge.

> "Everyone keeps saying the gods choose. I want to know if they choose well."

Choose one test:

| Test | Check |
|---|---|
| Poison cup test | Constitution save or Medicine check, DC 12 |
| Shadow tag | Perception, Stealth, or Acrobatics, DC 12 |
| Sparring match | Athletics, Acrobatics, or attack roll |
| Nerve test | Wisdom save or Insight check, DC 12 |

Joka's reaction:

- Success: "Good. I prefer rivals who do not embarrass me."
- Failure: "Interesting. Weakness tells the truth faster than strength."
- Refusal: "Better. You may not be trained after all."

### Master Seigen

If the player visits or passes the Medicine Hall, Seigen watches them closely.

> "You look pale. Fear, devotion, or guilt?"

If the player mentions his secret:

> "Careful. Some truths are venomous even before spoken."

### Master Ye Sol

Ye Sol gives practical advice.

> "Do not choose the altar that flatters you. Choose the one whose burden you can carry when no one thanks you."

## Scene 3: The Three Bow Ceremony

The temple gathers in the Hall of Lamps.

The ceremony has three parts:

1. Bow to the Temple — honoring those who raised you.
2. Bow to the Gods — accepting divine service.
3. Bow to the Chain — accepting sacrifice.

### Iwamoto's Ceremony Speech

> "Discipline is not obedience. Compassion is not softness. Service is not slavery.
>
> Remember this when the chain tightens.
>
> A chain may bind. A chain may also hold the world together.
>
> Enter one at a time. Leave as yourselves, or as what the world requires you to become."

## Scene 4: The Hall of Lamps

Thousands of lamps hang in the darkness. Only three altars are lit.

| Altar | Vision | God |
|---|---|---|
| Bone cradle filled with flowers | A mountain breathing like a sleeping beast | Father of Monsters |
| Silver spear beneath a red moon | A forest running away from the hunter | Storjeger |
| Black serpent biting its own shadow | An ocean whispering names backward | Zehir |

### Player Visions

**Father of Monsters:** The player stands on a mountain of bone. Beneath the mountain, something breathes. Ask: "When the mountain wakes, what do you fear it will remember about you?"

**Storjeger:** The player runs through a moonlit forest. The trees flee alongside them. Something is hunting the entire world. Ask: "Do you hide, run, or turn toward the hunter?"

**Zehir:** The player floats beneath a black ocean. Voices whisper names backward. One voice is theirs. Ask: "What secret does the ocean try to steal from you?"

### NPC Visions

Yuko chooses Storjeger. For one heartbeat, her shadow has the shape of a wolf running on two legs.

Joka chooses Zehir. The lamps nearest her turn blue-black.

Jon Soo does not place his hand on any altar. If challenged later, he says, "Some chains are private."

## Scene 5: Seal-Warped Beast Attack

### Read-Aloud

The final lamp gutters.

For one breath, every flame in the Hall of Lamps leans west.

Then something screams outside.

You rush into the courtyard. Prayer flags tear loose from their ropes. Students scatter. A black-furred beast drags itself over the outer wall, its jaw split too wide, its body twitching as though several creatures are trying to wear the same skin.

Master Ye Sol shouts, "Younger students behind me!"

Jon Soo's drunken smile disappears.

Yuko's hand finds a hidden blade.

Joka smiles like she has been waiting for the world to admit what it is.

Grandmaster Iwamoto does not move.

His eyes are fixed on you.

### Encounter

For a solo player with Jon Soo, Yuko, and Joka, use:

- 3 Seal-Warped Beasts
- Base creatures: wolves, panthers, or giant rats
- Each beast has one mutation

| d6 | Mutation |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Stone Plates: +1 AC |
| 2 | Sliver Twitch: Advantage on initiative |
| 3 | Poison Breath: once per fight, 10-foot cone, DC 11 Constitution save, 1d6 poison damage on failure, half on success |
| 4 | Memory Echo: speaks one sentence from the player's childhood |
| 5 | Hunger Aura: creatures within 5 feet cannot regain hit points |
| 6 | Split Jaw: bite deals +2 damage |

### NPC Combat Behavior

Jon Soo engages the largest beast and protects the player.

Yuko flanks, scouts weak points, and supports the player.

Joka poisons or disables enemies and may finish one brutally.

### Important Moment

One beast should have Memory Echo. It speaks a sentence from the player's childhood, then turns toward Grandmaster Iwamoto and says in his voice:

> "Begin with fear."

## Scene 6: The Prayer Tag

After the fight, the player finds a broken temple prayer tag tied around one beast's neck.

It is written in Grandmaster Iwamoto's private hand.

> "Begin with fear. Let them believe the world needs them."

### Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Investigation | 11 | The tag was tied deliberately |
| Religion | 12 | The prayer script is protective, but inverted |
| Insight | 13 | The phrase sounds like an instruction, not a prayer |
| History | 14 | The calligraphy resembles old temple founding documents |

If the player confronts Iwamoto, he does not deny the handwriting.

> "A cruel phrase. An old one. It was used in training during darker generations. Fear can ruin a student. It can also reveal one."

This is technically true.

## Scene 7: Aftermath

The temple tends its wounded. Master Ye Sol is furious that the beasts reached the courtyard. Seigen quietly examines venom traces. Yuko stays close to the player. Joka watches everyone's reactions. Jon Soo drinks alone under the eaves.

### Private Beats

Yuko: "When that thing spoke from your childhood, I wanted to kill it twice."

Joka: "Someone wanted us afraid. That does not mean they are our enemy. But it does mean they think we are tools."

Jon Soo: "Old men do not write sentences like that unless they have already forgiven themselves."

### Session End

Grandmaster Iwamoto addresses the player and NPC party.

> "Tonight, you saw a crack in the world. Tomorrow, you begin learning how to hold it closed."

Milestone: the player advances to level 2.
