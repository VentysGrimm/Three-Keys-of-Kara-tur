# 09 - Location: Shrine of the Buried Bell

## Foundry Journal Target

`Act I / Locations / Shrine of the Buried Bell`

## Location Role

The Shrine of the Buried Bell is the first dungeon-like site in the campaign. It teaches the player that seals are physical, local, fragile, and historically edited. It also introduces the Tarrasque seal through dreams, bells, stone, and sleeping earth.

## Read-Aloud: Exterior

The shrine leans into the hillside as though the mountain has been slowly eating it.

Roots curl over roof tiles. Moss fills cracks in the stone steps. Two fox statues flank the entrance, but their faces have been chiseled away so violently that the stone around their necks is still scarred.

From somewhere below comes a soft bronze sound.

Not a ringing.

A heartbeat.

## GM Summary

The shrine was built over a minor resonance point connected to the Tarrasque's sleep. Its buried bell once dampened nightmares and earth tremors. The bell has cracked, its clapper has been swallowed, and the Bell-Eater Grub now feeds on the seal's failing resonance.

The dungeon should feel old, cramped, root-choked, and sonically wrong.

## Dungeon Features

- Ceilings are low and cracked.
- Roots grow through walls and floors.
- Old prayer strips hang blackened and brittle.
- Sound behaves strangely.
- Whispers echo loudly, but footsteps are muffled.
- Metal objects vibrate faintly.
- Bronze dust gathers in corners.
- Tremors occur when the Bell-Eater Grub moves.

## Area 1: Root-Choked Gate

### Read-Aloud

The entrance is blocked by roots wrapped around old iron prayer chains. Each chain bears a bronze tag stamped with a bell symbol. Several tags have split open from the inside, as if the sound within them became too large.

### Challenge

The party must open the gate.

| Method | Check | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Pull roots apart | Athletics DC 13 | Opens the gate loudly |
| Cut the correct roots | Nature DC 12 | Opens the gate quietly |
| Recite opening sutra | Religion DC 12 | Calms the bell resonance |
| Pick chain-locks | Thieves' Tools DC 13 | Opens without damaging the shrine |
| Burn the roots | No check | Opens gate but triggers nightmare resonance |

### Failure

A small cave-in occurs. Each nearby creature makes a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw or takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage.

### NPC Assistance

- Jon Soo can help force the gate.
- Yuko can find a side gap.
- Joka can identify poisonous root growth.
- The player should make the final decision.

## Area 2: Hall of Sleeping Teeth

### Read-Aloud

The first chamber is painted with a mural so old that the colors have faded into the stone.

A mountain rises at the center of the wall. Beneath it, something vast sleeps with teeth the size of temple gates. Around the mountain stand divine figures, each with a hand extended toward a chain of light.

One figure has been scratched away.

Not weathered.

Removed.

### Mural Clues

Original sequence:

1. The Father of Monsters kneels beside the sleeping Tarrasque.
2. Three divine figures bind three disasters.
3. A fourth figure stands behind them.
4. The fourth figure has been scratched out.

The scratched-out figure has faint traces of nine tails.

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Investigation | 12 | The scratches were intentional, not accidental damage |
| Religion | 13 | The scratched figure was not originally painted as a demon |
| History | 14 | Older regional shrine art sometimes included helper spirits beside gods |
| Perception | 15 | Recent plaster hides additional fox-tail marks |

### Secret

This is one of the first physical proofs that Atsumori Nine-Tails was erased from divine-seal history.

## Area 3: Cracked Bell Chamber

### Read-Aloud

The chamber opens into a circular pit room. A cracked bronze bell hangs upside down over the darkness. Its clapper is missing.

The walls are covered in old sealing sutras written so tightly that the characters overlap like woven chain.

When you breathe, the bell answers.

Very softly.

### Hazard: Nightmare Resonance

When a creature speaks above a whisper, strikes metal, casts thunder magic, or violently shakes the chamber, the bell vibrates.

Each creature in the room must make a DC 11 Wisdom saving throw.

On a failure, the creature sees a brief nightmare of the Tarrasque waking and has disadvantage on its next ability check inside the shrine.

### Mark-Specific Omens

- **Bone Cradle:** "Not dead. Dreaming."
- **Silver Spear:** Distant horns flee the mountain.
- **Black Serpent:** "Even stone can be poisoned."

### Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Religion | 13 | The bell was used to suppress dreams, not summon spirits |
| Arcana | 14 | The bell's resonance is being consumed by something below |
| Investigation | 13 | The missing clapper was torn free from below |
| Medicine | 14 | The nightmares are causing physical exhaustion similar to poison |

## Area 4: Feeding Tunnel

### Read-Aloud

The tunnel below the bell chamber is slick with pale slime and bronze powder.

Goat bones lie crushed beside cracked prayer beads. Strips of old sutra paper have been chewed into wet pulp. Something heavy drags itself through the dark ahead, and with every movement, the walls answer in a low bronze hum.

### Signs

- Crushed goat skulls.
- Bronze shavings in dung.
- Prayer beads embedded in slime.
- A tunnel leading beneath the bell chamber.
- Bell tones vibrating through the floor.

### Approach

A DC 12 Survival or Stealth check allows the party to approach quietly.

If the party fails, the Bell-Eater Grub begins combat in a favorable position.

## Boss Area: Bell-Eater Grub Chamber

Use this chamber for the Bell-Eater Grub boss fight.

### Terrain

- Cracked bronze clapper embedded in grub's body.
- Loose bones count as difficult terrain.
- Bell chains hang overhead.
- Root columns provide half cover.
- A shallow pit drops 10 feet near the center.

### Environmental Options

| Option | Check | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Swing from bell chain | Acrobatics DC 13 | Move across pit without provoking |
| Pull root column | Athletics DC 14 | Drops debris, dealing 1d6 bludgeoning in a 5-foot area |
| Strike exposed bronze clapper | Attack vs AC 14 | Disrupts the grub's resonance until next turn |
| Recite sealing breath | Religion DC 13 | Prevents Bell-Clapper Scream from affecting one ally |
| Scatter bronze powder | Sleight of Hand DC 13 | Confuses grub tremorsense until end of round |

## Bell-Eater Grub Summary

Large monstrosity, unaligned.

- AC 14
- HP 60
- Speed 30 ft., burrow 10 ft.
- Tremorsense 30 ft.
- Bite: +5 to hit, 1d8 + 3 piercing
- Spasm: nearby Dexterity save or 1d6 bludgeoning and push
- Bloodied Reaction: Bell-Clapper Scream, DC 12 Wisdom save or frightened until end of next turn

Link to the full Actor when built.

## Reward: First Bell Fragment

When the grub dies, the missing clapper splits open. Inside is a cracked bronze shard warm with a heartbeat-like pulse.

The First Bell Fragment is both a reward and a ritual component.

### Mechanical Note

Once before the end of Act II, the player may ring the fragment as an action. Hostile creatures within 15 feet must make a DC 12 Wisdom save or be unable to take reactions until the start of the player's next turn.

After this use, the fragment goes silent until reforged during a later ritual.

## Secrets to Reveal

1. The bell was built to keep something sleeping.
2. The shrine art once included a fourth fox-marked figure.
3. Someone recently covered parts of the mural.
4. The Bell-Eater Grub is a symptom, not the original cause.
5. The failed minor seal points toward the greater Tarrasque seal.
