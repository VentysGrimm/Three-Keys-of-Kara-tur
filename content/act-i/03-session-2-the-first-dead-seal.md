# 03 - Session 2: The First Dead Seal

## Session Purpose

Send the player beyond the temple, show common people suffering from a failed minor seal, reveal altered divine history, and recover the First Bell Fragment.

## Traveling Party

- Player character
- Jon Soo
- Yuko Yanagi
- Joka

Master Ye Sol and Master Seigen remain at the temple.

## Scene 1: Iwamoto's Assignment

### Read-Aloud

Morning mist clings to the training yard. The broken stones from yesterday's attack have already been swept into neat piles.

Grandmaster Iwamoto waits beside a low table. On it rests a map, a travel satchel, and four cups of untouched tea.

"The great seals do not break all at once," he says. "They whisper first. They trouble dreams. They sour wells. They teach beasts to pray."

He taps one finger on the map.

"A small shrine below the western ridge has gone silent. You four will go together."

His eyes settle on you.

"And you will listen most carefully."

## Scene 2: The Road to Ash Millet

The path descends through cedar forest, old terraces, cracked stone markers, and abandoned wayside shrines.

Use 1–2 travel complications.

| d6 | Travel Complication |
|---:|---|
| 1 | A stone lantern has fallen uphill, not downhill |
| 2 | Birds are silent for one full mile |
| 3 | A deer bows toward the party, then collapses dead |
| 4 | A tremor opens a narrow crack in the road |
| 5 | The party hears a bell underground, though no bell is visible |
| 6 | A child's voice calls the player by name from inside a sealed roadside shrine |

### NPC Reactions

- Yuko becomes alert and tracks invisible movement.
- Joka checks the air for poison or spirit residue.
- Jon Soo jokes until something suggests a massacre or deliberate cruelty.

## Scene 3: Village of Ash Millet

Ash Millet is a poor mountain village built among steep fields and old stone walls.

### Village Problems

- Livestock have vanished.
- Farmers dream of a mountain breathing beneath them.
- Wells taste of iron.
- Tremors have cracked grain jars and ancestor tablets.
- At night, something rings beneath the earth.

### Key Villagers

**Elder Hana** is practical, exhausted, and suspicious of temple promises.

> "You monks always come after the shaking starts. Never before."

**Toma, Goat-Herder Child** saw something drag a goat toward the shrine path.

> "It had too many legs, but it rang like festival bronze."

**Old Man Ren** keeps forbidden local stories. If persuaded:

> "My grandfather said the bell was not built to wake gods. It was built to keep one sleeping."

### Party Roleplay

Yuko comforts frightened children and studies tracks. A child may ask the player, "Is she your wife?" Yuko smiles and watches the player's reaction.

Joka tastes the well water and says, "Fear makes peasants honest for one breath, then stupid for the rest." If challenged, she adds, "I did not say they deserve harm. I said fear makes them useful."

Jon Soo drinks with farmers, jokes with elders, and quietly asks who vanished first. If he finds evidence of slaughter, his humor vanishes.

### Useful Checks

| Check | DC | Information |
|---|---:|---|
| Persuasion | 11 | Missing livestock began three nights ago |
| Medicine | 12 | Villagers' nightmares are causing real exhaustion |
| Nature | 12 | No normal predator made the tracks |
| Religion | 13 | Village prayers are being pulled toward the mountain shrine |
| Survival | 13 | Tracks lead to the Shrine of the Buried Bell |
| Insight | 13 | Elder Hana is afraid the temple caused this |

At some point, a villager asks the player:

> "Are you here to save us, or to make us feel saved?"

This should echo the Grandmaster's manipulation.

# The Shrine of the Buried Bell

The shrine is half-swallowed by roots and stone. Inside is a cracked bronze bell that once suppressed dreams of the Tarrasque.

## Scene 4: Arrival at the Shrine

### Read-Aloud

The shrine leans into the hillside as though the mountain has been slowly eating it. Roots curl over the roof tiles. Stone foxes flank the entrance, but their faces have been chiseled away.

From somewhere below comes a soft bronze sound.

Not a ringing.

A heartbeat.

### Shrine Features

- Ceilings are low and cracked.
- Roots grow through walls.
- Old prayer strips hang blackened and brittle.
- Sound behaves strangely.
- Metal objects vibrate faintly.
- Whispers echo loudly, but footsteps are muffled.

## Area 1: Root-Choked Gate

The entrance is blocked by roots wrapped around old prayer chains.

| Method | Check |
|---|---|
| Pull roots apart | Athletics DC 13 |
| Cut the correct roots | Nature DC 12 |
| Recite opening sutra | Religion DC 12 |
| Pick chain-locks | Thieves' Tools DC 13 |

Failure causes a small cave-in. Each nearby creature makes a DC 11 Dexterity save or takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage.

NPC assistance:

- Jon Soo can help force the gate.
- Yuko can find a narrow side gap.
- Joka can identify a poisonous root growth.
- The player should make the final choice.

## Area 2: Hall of Sleeping Teeth

Murals show the Father of Monsters sealing a colossal beast beneath a mountain.

A successful DC 12 Investigation or History check reveals the murals have been altered.

Original sequence:

1. The Father of Monsters kneels beside the sleeping Tarrasque.
2. Three divine figures bind three disasters.
3. A fourth figure stands behind them.
4. The fourth figure has been scratched out.

The scratched-out figure has faint traces of nine tails.

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Religion | 13 | The scratched figure was not a demon in the original mural |
| History | 14 | Older temple art often included helper spirits beside gods |
| Investigation | 15 | The scratches are old, but some recent plaster covers the same area |

Do not reveal Atsumori yet unless the player has already gathered strong evidence.

## Area 3: Cracked Bell Chamber

A cracked bronze bell hangs upside down over a pit. The clapper is missing.

The walls are covered with old sealing sutras.

### Hazard: Nightmare Resonance

When a creature speaks above a whisper, the bell vibrates.

Each creature in the room must make a DC 11 Wisdom save. On a failure, they see a brief nightmare of the Tarrasque waking and have disadvantage on their next ability check inside the shrine.

- Father of Monsters mark: "Not dead. Dreaming."
- Storjeger mark: distant horns fleeing the mountain.
- Zehir mark: "Even stone can be poisoned."

## Area 4: Feeding Tunnel

The Bell-Eater Grub has dragged livestock bones here.

Signs include:

- Crushed goat skulls.
- Bronze shavings in dung.
- Prayer beads embedded in slime.
- A tunnel leading beneath the bell chamber.

A DC 12 Survival check lets the party approach quietly.

NPC reactions:

- Yuko: "It hunts by vibration."
- Joka: "No. It feeds by vibration. There is a difference."
- Jon Soo: "I preferred it when monsters just had teeth."

# Boss Encounter: Bell-Eater Grub

Large monstrosity, unaligned.

Use this boss for a level 2 solo-player party with three NPC allies.

## Stat Profile

- **Armor Class:** 14
- **Hit Points:** 60
- **Speed:** 30 ft., burrow 10 ft.
- **STR:** 16
- **DEX:** 10
- **CON:** 15
- **INT:** 2
- **WIS:** 10
- **CHA:** 5
- **Saving Throws:** Con +4
- **Skills:** Perception +2
- **Senses:** Tremorsense 30 ft., passive Perception 12

## Traits

**Bronze-Fed Body.** The grub has advantage on saving throws against being poisoned.

**Ringing Flesh.** The first time each round the grub takes bludgeoning or thunder damage, each creature within 10 feet hears a painful bell tone. Those creatures must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution save or take 1 thunder damage.

## Actions

**Bite.** Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d8 + 3 piercing damage.

**Spasm.** Each creature within 5 feet must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity save or take 1d6 bludgeoning damage and be pushed 5 feet.

## Bloodied Reaction

**Bell-Clapper Scream.** When reduced to half hit points or fewer, the grub rings from inside.

Each creature within 30 feet must make a DC 12 Wisdom save. On a failure, the creature is frightened until the end of its next turn. A creature that succeeds is immune to this effect for 24 hours.

## Boss Spotlight Mechanic

At the start of round 3, the swallowed bell-clapper rings from inside the grub. The player sees a glowing crack in its bronze-swollen body.

| Action | Check | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Strike the glowing crack | Attack roll vs AC 14 | Extra damage and weakens the fear ring |
| Recite a sealing breath | Religion DC 13 | Grub loses its reaction |
| Distract it for Jon Soo | Performance or Acrobatics DC 13 | Jon Soo gets a free strike |
| Signal Yuko | Insight or Perception DC 13 | Yuko exposes a weak point |
| Trust Joka's poison | Medicine or Sleight of Hand DC 13 | Joka's poison bypasses resistance |

## Tactics

The grub attacks whoever creates the most vibration, pushes enemies into walls or pits, burrows away if surrounded, and attacks frightened prey after its bloodied scream.

## Scaling

If the player is struggling, reduce HP to 45 and remove Spasm.

If the player is optimized, increase HP to 70 and add two Seal-Warped giant rats on round 2.

## Reward: The First Bell Fragment

When the grub dies, its body splits open. Inside is the missing bell-clapper, cracked in half and fused with a bronze shard.

The fragment is warm. It faintly beats like a heart.

### Minor Power

Once before the end of Act II, the player may ring it as an action. Each hostile creature within 15 feet must make a DC 12 Wisdom save or be unable to take reactions until the start of the player's next turn.

After this use, the fragment goes silent until reforged during a later ritual.

### Story Use

The First Bell Fragment becomes part of the final sealing ritual.

## Scene 5: Return to Ash Millet

The village feels lighter after the grub dies. The tremors stop. The wells clear by morning. Livestock no longer panic.

Elder Hana thanks the party, but not with blind faith.

> "You did good. Remember that when old men in clean robes tell you what your good is worth."

NPC reactions:

- Yuko is quiet on the road back, troubled by the scratched-out fox.
- Joka asks whether the player thinks the temple lied intentionally or accidentally.
- Jon Soo says, "Accidental lies do not usually come with fresh plaster."

Milestone: the player advances to level 3 after recovering the First Bell Fragment.
