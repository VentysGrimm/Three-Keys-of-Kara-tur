# 04 - Session 3: The Grandmaster's Test

## Session Purpose

Return to the temple, test the party's willingness to sacrifice themselves, deepen NPC secrets, reveal altered histories, and end Act I with the map to the wider campaign.

## Scene 1: Return to the Temple

### Read-Aloud

When you return, the temple bells ring once.

Not celebration. Recognition.

Students gather in the courtyard. Some look at your wounds before they look at your faces. Master Ye Sol stands with arms folded, her tattoos blooming dark green along her wrists.

Grandmaster Iwamoto descends the steps slowly.

"You have heard the world cracking," he says. "And you returned carrying one of its broken sounds."

His eyes move to the Bell Fragment.

"How heavy is it?"

## Scene 2: Private Reactions

Give the player time with each NPC.

### Yuko

Yuko asks to walk with the player near the prayer flags.

> "In the shrine, when I saw the scratched-out fox, I felt like something was watching from behind my own eyes."

If the player comforts her, she almost reveals something about being Lythari, then stops.

### Joka

Joka waits in the Moon Gate Courtyard.

> "You saw it too. The fox was removed. Not defeated. Removed. That is not history. That is surgery."

If the player agrees, she becomes more respectful. If the player dismisses it, she says:

> "Then I hope blindness is part of your vow."

### Jon Soo

Jon Soo sits on the roof, looking toward the mountains.

> "Big monsters are honest. People who point you at them usually are not."

If the player asks what he means:

> "When something huge wants to kill you, it does not usually ask to be called Grandmaster first."

### Master Ye Sol

Ye Sol examines the player's injuries.

> "You survived. Good. Now tell me what you saw that you were not supposed to see."

If the player shares the fox mural or prayer tag, she becomes grim.

> "I knew he was hiding a blade. I did not know it was pointed at history."

## Scene 3: The Grandmaster's Question

Grandmaster Iwamoto summons the player and the NPC party.

He asks the player first:

> "Would you die to preserve the seal?"

| Player Answer | Iwamoto's Response |
|---|---|
| Yes | "May you never be asked honestly." |
| No | "Good. A life thrown away too easily has little weight." |
| I do not know | "The only truthful answer." |
| I would find another way | "Then you have begun where most masters end." |
| Only for those I love | "The world will try to become someone you love." |

Then he asks the NPCs.

### Yuko's Answer

> "For the world? I don't know. For someone I loved? Yes."

She does not look at the player immediately, but she wants to.

### Joka's Answer

> "I would kill to preserve it. Dying is less useful."

A nearby flame darkens.

### Jon Soo's Answer

> "I already died for something once. It did not help."

Iwamoto smiles sadly.

> "No. But it taught you what a living corpse can do."

Jon Soo's expression hardens. This hints that Iwamoto knows Jon Soo's secret.

## Scene 4: Master Seigen's Warning

That night, the player receives a message written on thin medicinal paper. The ink smells of bitter herbs.

> "The histories are poisoned. Come to the western archive after moonrise. Come alone if you are brave. Bring allies if you are wise."

If the player shows the party:

- Yuko: "That is a trap. But not necessarily one meant to kill you."
- Joka: "It is Master Seigen. If he wanted you dead, the paper would have done it."
- Jon Soo: "I hate clever old men. Present company included."

## Scene 5: The Western Archive

The western archive is a tall wooden hall built into the cliffside. It contains scroll towers, rotating shelves, prayer tablets, tax records, monster lore, forbidden sutras, and censored histories.

### Read-Aloud

The western archive smells of cedar dust, lamp oil, and medicinal smoke.

Scroll shelves climb three stories into shadow. Thin bridges connect the upper stacks. Thousands of prayer tablets hang from red cords, clicking softly though there is no wind.

Master Seigen stands beside a reading table covered in scrolls. His hands are folded inside his sleeves. A single cup of tea waits untouched beside him.

"You saw something you should not have," he says.

His eyes remain human.

Mostly.

"The question is whether you understood it."

### What Seigen Reveals

Seigen reveals enough to move the campaign forward, but not everything he knows.

1. The temple histories have been rewritten.
2. The three seals were not maintained by gods alone.
3. A fourth figure once stood beside the divine seal-keepers.
4. That figure has been removed from nearly every record.
5. Grandmaster Iwamoto's hand appears on recent alterations.
6. Some sealed records are not in the archive, but beneath the temple.

### Why Seigen Helps

Choose one or more:

- He wants leverage over Iwamoto.
- He wants to protect Joka from being used as a sacrifice.
- He believes Zehir's seal is weakening faster than the others.
- He wants to test whether the player is useful.
- He suspects the player may expose him and wants control of the situation.

If the player accuses Seigen:

> "Do not mistake my secrets for his. Mine are smaller, cleaner, and better labeled."

## Scene 6: Masked Temple Guardians Attack

Before Seigen can explain everything, masked temple guardians enter the archive.

They wear blank white masks. They are not trying to kill the party. They are testing, frightening, and separating them.

- **Who sent them?** Grandmaster Iwamoto arranged the attack.
- **Who expected them?** Master Seigen suspected they might come.
- **What do the guardians believe?** Most believe this is a sanctioned loyalty test. They do not know Iwamoto's true identity.

Use guard, bandit, cultist, or martial artist-style stat blocks. Use 4–6 guardians depending on how capable the player and NPCs are.

Give each guardian one trait.

| Trait | Effect |
|---|---|
| Hooked chain | Can attempt to grapple at 10 feet |
| Smoke pellet | Once, creates light obscurement in a 10-foot area |
| Prayer seal | Once, imposes disadvantage on a spell attack or concentration check |
| Staff fighter | +1 AC while wielding staff |
| Silent step | Advantage on Stealth checks |
| Venom needle | On hit, target must make DC 11 Con save or lose reactions for 1 round |

### NPC Reactions

- Jon Soo immediately moves between the player and the attackers.
- Yuko extinguishes lamps and creates escape routes.
- Joka recognizes one guardian's poison technique and realizes Seigen trained at least some of them.
- Seigen calmly watches to see how the player reacts.

If confronted, Seigen says:

> "A lesson can be an ambush. An ambush can be a diagnosis."

# Skill Challenge: Escape the Archive

The party must get 5 successes before 3 failures.

The player should roll for most major decisions. NPCs provide support, grant advantage, or absorb consequences.

## Suggested Checks

| Skill | Use |
|---|---|
| Acrobatics | Leap between shelves or swing from prayer cords |
| Athletics | Hold a collapsing archive door or shove a shelf into pursuers |
| History | Identify the oldest exit routes |
| Investigation | Find the hidden fox-marked scroll case |
| Religion | Recognize a false prayer seal |
| Stealth | Avoid temple guardians |
| Perception | Hear guardians moving above or below |
| Arcana | Disrupt a warded archive lock |
| Medicine | Identify Seigen's venom-lock mechanism |

DCs:

- DC 12 for straightforward actions.
- DC 14 for risky or clever actions.
- DC 16 for desperate actions under pressure.

## NPC Support Options

**Jon Soo: Drunken Diversion.** Jon Soo knocks over a shelf, laughs loudly, and draws guardians away. Cancel one failure or give advantage on Athletics, Acrobatics, or Deception.

**Yuko: Moonlit Path.** Yuko finds a silent upper walkway through the archive. Give advantage on Stealth, Perception, or Survival.

**Joka: Poisoned Needle.** Joka disables a pursuing guardian with a precise toxin. Remove one guardian complication or give advantage on Sleight of Hand, Medicine, or Stealth.

**Master Seigen: Serpent's Key.** Seigen opens a sealed scroll case with a drop of venom. Automatically reveal the fox-marked scroll case, but the player now owes Seigen a favor.

## Failure Complications

| d6 | Complication |
|---:|---|
| 1 | A shelf collapses, splitting the party |
| 2 | A guardian seizes the First Bell Fragment |
| 3 | Yuko is injured protecting the player |
| 4 | Joka kills a guardian too quickly, escalating the scene |
| 5 | Jon Soo nearly loses control of his hidden oath |
| 6 | Seigen abandons the group temporarily to protect his own secrets |

## Success Outcome

The party escapes with:

- An old map of Kara-Tur.
- A fox-marked scroll case.
- Partial proof that temple history was rewritten.
- Evidence connecting Iwamoto to the alterations.

## Failure Outcome

The party still escapes, but at a cost:

- The Bell Fragment is temporarily taken.
- Yuko is seriously injured.
- Joka is blamed for killing a guardian.
- Jon Soo's secret mask is seen.
- Seigen demands a favor immediately.
- Master Ye Sol must cover for the party.

Do not let failure stop the campaign.

# The Fox-Marked Scroll Case

The scroll case is black lacquer with a barely visible fox mark under the dust.

Inside are three things.

## 1. The Old Map of Kara-Tur

The map marks three destinations:

| Location | Lore |
|---|---|
| The Bone-Maw Mountains | Tarrasque lore |
| The Moon-Hunted Forest | Sliver lore |
| The Black Glass Coast | Aboleth lore |

A fourth mark sits beneath the Temple of the Dawn Chain. It is not labeled.

## 2. A Damaged Founding Sutra

Most of it is unreadable, but these lines remain:

> "Three gods set hand to chain."
>
> "Three horrors sank beneath vow, fang, moon, and venom."
>
> "The fourth taught breath to mortal flesh."
>
> "The fourth must not be named after the betrayal."

A successful DC 14 Religion or History check reveals that "breath to mortal flesh" likely refers to the living sutra rites.

## 3. A Recent Copy in Iwamoto's Hand

This copy removes all references to "the fourth."

In the margin, written in Grandmaster Iwamoto's private hand:

> "They must inherit the lie before they can break it."

This should complicate the mystery. It suggests Iwamoto may want the party to uncover the truth eventually.

That is true.

He wants them strong, suspicious, and desperate enough to seek the seals.

# Scene 7: End of Act I Choices

## Option A: Hide the Evidence

The party keeps the map secret and pretends nothing happened.

At breakfast, Grandmaster Iwamoto smiles and says:

> "Secrets are heavy. Eat well."

## Option B: Confront Iwamoto

Iwamoto does not confess or attack.

> "Every temple is built on buried things. The question is whether the foundation holds."

If shown the map, he confirms that the three marked sites are real and dangerous. He encourages the player to go.

## Option C: Go to Master Ye Sol

Ye Sol believes the player reluctantly.

> "I knew he was hiding a blade. I did not know it was pointed at history."

She can help the party leave safely.

## Option D: Trust Master Seigen

Seigen offers to help interpret the map, but only if the player agrees to keep his secrets.

> "Trust is sentimental. Leverage is reliable. Fortunately, we now have both."

## Option E: Investigate the Fourth Mark Immediately

If the player insists on going beneath the temple now, allow them to find only the upper entrance to the sealed lower stair.

The door bears four symbols:

- Bone cradle
- Silver spear
- Black serpent
- Nine-tailed fox

The fox symbol has been burned black.

The stair cannot yet be opened without knowledge from the three marked sites.

# Final Act I Revelation

The player has an old map of Kara-Tur marked with three distant destinations:

- The Bone-Maw Mountains
- The Moon-Hunted Forest
- The Black Glass Coast

And one mark beneath home:

- The Temple of the Dawn Chain

The temple is not merely guarding the path to the seals.

The temple is one of the seals' secrets.

## Closing Read-Aloud

The map is older than the temple stones, older than the current dynasty, older perhaps than the names of the gods as you learned them.

Three marks wait at the edges of Kara-Tur.

Bone. Moon. Glass.

Yuko's hand lingers near the mark of the Moon-Hunted Forest.
Joka watches the Black Glass Coast as if it has whispered an insult.
Jon Soo's smile fades when he sees the Bone-Maw Mountains.
Master Seigen studies none of them.

He studies you.

Then your eyes return to the fourth mark.

Not in a distant ruin.

Not across a poisoned sea.

Not beneath a monster's mountain.

Here.

Under the Temple of the Dawn Chain.

Above you, the monastery bells begin to ring for morning prayer.

For the first time in your life, they do not sound holy.

They sound like something counting down.
