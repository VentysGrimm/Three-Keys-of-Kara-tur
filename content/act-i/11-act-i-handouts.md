# 11 - Act I Handouts

## Foundry Journal Target

`Act I / Handouts / Act I Handouts`

## Handout Use

These entries should become player-facing handouts or Item descriptions in Foundry. Keep GM-only commentary out of the player versions.

## Handout 1: Prayer Tag in Iwamoto's Hand

### Player-Facing Text

A broken temple prayer tag, torn at one corner and tied with black thread.

The calligraphy is elegant and old-fashioned. The ink has bled where beast saliva touched the paper.

The surviving line reads:

> "Begin with fear. Let them believe the world needs them."

### Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Investigation | 11 | The tag was tied deliberately |
| Religion | 12 | The prayer script is protective, but inverted |
| Insight | 13 | The phrase sounds like an instruction, not a prayer |
| History | 14 | The calligraphy resembles old temple founding documents |

### GM Notes

This is the first clue that Iwamoto arranged or anticipated the beast attack. Do not over-explain. Let the player sit with the contradiction between his kindness and the cruelty of the phrase.

## Handout 2: First Bell Fragment

### Player-Facing Text

A cracked bronze fragment taken from the body of the Bell-Eater Grub.

It was once part of a bell clapper. It is warm to the touch, and when held near the ear, it gives a faint sound like a heartbeat beneath stone.

The surface bears old sutra marks, half-chewed and partly melted into the bronze.

### Minor Power

Once before the end of Act II, you may ring the fragment as an action. Each hostile creature within 15 feet must make a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature cannot take reactions until the start of your next turn.

After this use, the fragment goes silent until reforged or awakened by a later ritual.

### GM Notes

The First Bell Fragment is a future ritual component. It should matter mechanically once, then become a story object until Act II or later.

## Handout 3: Old Map of Kara-Tur

### Player-Facing Text

An old map painted on treated silk. The ink has faded to brown and gray, but four marks remain visible.

Three marks sit far from the Temple of the Dawn Chain:

- A bone-shaped mountain mark.
- A red moon over a black forest.
- A black glass wave along the coast.

The fourth mark is not far away.

It sits beneath the Temple of the Dawn Chain.

It has no label.

### Player Clue

The three distant marks appear to point toward the next stage of the pilgrimage.

### GM Notes

The three marks correspond to the Act II destination arcs:

- Bone-Maw Mountains
- Moon-Hunted Forest
- Black Glass Coast

The fourth mark points to the hidden seal below the temple, which cannot be opened yet.

## Handout 4: Damaged Founding Sutra

### Player-Facing Text

A damaged sutra written on old paper, brittle at the edges and darkened by smoke.

Most of the text is unreadable. A few lines remain:

> "Three gods set hand to chain."
>
> "Three horrors sank beneath vow, fang, moon, and venom."
>
> "The fourth taught breath to mortal flesh."
>
> "The fourth must not be named after the betrayal."

### Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| Religion | 14 | "Breath to mortal flesh" likely refers to the living sutra rites |
| History | 14 | This wording predates current temple doctrine |
| Investigation | 15 | Some missing fragments were cut out, not lost to age |

### GM Notes

This is the strongest Act I evidence that a fourth figure existed in the founding history.

## Handout 5: Recent Copy in Iwamoto's Hand

### Player-Facing Text

A recent copy of the damaged founding sutra, written in a steady and elegant hand.

The copied text removes all references to "the fourth."

In the margin, the same hand has written:

> "They must inherit the lie before they can break it."

### Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---:|---|
| History | 13 | The writing style resembles other temple records |
| Investigation | 13 | The copy was made recently |
| Insight | 14 | The margin note sounds regretful, not triumphant |
| Religion | 15 | The removal changes the theological meaning of the sutra |

### GM Notes

This clue complicates Iwamoto. He is not merely hiding the truth. He is staging when and how it will be discovered.

## Handout 6: Fox-Marked Scroll Case

### Player-Facing Text

A black lacquer scroll case, old but carefully preserved.

Dust covers most of the surface. Beneath the grime is a faint mark: a fox with too many tails, almost rubbed away.

The latch does not open with force. It opens only when the case is turned so the fox faces east.

Inside are an old map, a damaged sutra, and a recent copy in familiar handwriting.

### GM Notes

This handout can be represented as either a JournalEntry or an Item. It should link to the three internal handouts:

- Old Map of Kara-Tur
- Damaged Founding Sutra
- Recent Copy in Iwamoto's Hand

## Handout 7: Divine Mark — Bone Cradle

### Player-Facing Text

Your vow leaves a subtle mark like pale bone beneath the skin.

Sometimes, when you stand on stone, you feel something far below breathe once in answer.

### Minor Mark

Once per long rest, when you are knocked prone or moved against your will, you may immediately reduce the forced movement by 10 feet or stand without spending movement.

### Burden

Animals sometimes flinch from you. Large stone structures seem to breathe when you are alone.

## Handout 8: Divine Mark — Silver Spear

### Player-Facing Text

Your vow leaves a faint silver line like moonlight across the skin.

Sometimes, when you look into a reflection, you see distant trees moving as if fleeing a hunter.

### Minor Mark

Once per long rest, you may add 1d4 to an initiative roll or Wisdom (Survival) check.

### Burden

Reflections sometimes show you being watched by something with too many eyes.

## Handout 9: Divine Mark — Black Serpent

### Player-Facing Text

Your vow leaves a dark mark like a serpent biting its own shadow.

Sometimes, still water ripples toward you though no wind touches it.

### Minor Mark

Once per long rest, you may gain advantage on a saving throw against poison or fear.

### Burden

You occasionally hear your name whispered backward by water, smoke, or sleep.
