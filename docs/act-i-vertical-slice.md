# Act I Vertical Slice: The Temple Lie

This document defines the first playable Foundry VTT vertical slice for **Three Keys of Kara-Tur**.

## Goal

Build a complete, testable Act I package for levels 1–3 before converting the full campaign.

The vertical slice should prove that the module can support:

- Solo-player D&D 5e play
- Rotating NPC companions
- Journal-driven adventure flow
- Linked Actors, Items, Roll Tables, and Scenes
- Encounter pacing for one player character plus NPC allies
- Campaign mysteries and relationship mechanics

## Act I Summary

**Act I — The Temple Lie** begins at the Temple of the Dawn Chain, where the player character and fellow students choose divine vows during the Three Bow Ceremony. The act reveals that the temple is both sacred home and engineered lie. The player uncovers evidence that Grandmaster Iwamoto is manipulating events, that temple history has been rewritten, and that a fourth fox-marked figure was erased from the founding story of the seals.

## Sessions

### Session 1: The Choosing of Chains

Purpose: introduce the temple, NPC companions, divine vows, first attack, and first clue against Iwamoto.

Key scenes:

1. Dawn at the Temple
2. Morning Interactions
3. The Three Bow Ceremony
4. The Hall of Lamps
5. Seal-Warped Beast Attack
6. The Prayer Tag
7. Aftermath

### Session 2: The First Dead Seal

Purpose: send the party beyond the temple, show civilians suffering from a failed minor seal, reveal altered divine history, and recover the First Bell Fragment.

Key scenes:

1. Iwamoto's Assignment
2. Road to Ash Millet
3. Village of Ash Millet
4. Shrine of the Buried Bell
5. Bell-Eater Grub boss encounter
6. Return to Ash Millet

### Session 3: The Grandmaster's Test

Purpose: test sacrifice, deepen secrets, reveal rewritten histories, and launch Act II.

Key scenes:

1. Return to the Temple
2. Private Reactions
3. The Grandmaster's Question
4. Master Seigen's Warning
5. Western Archive
6. Masked Temple Guardians Attack
7. Escape the Archive skill challenge
8. Fox-Marked Scroll Case
9. End of Act I choices

## Acceptance Criteria

Act I is considered playable when:

- A GM can run all three sessions using only the Act I journals.
- The player can choose one of three divine vows and receive a mechanical minor mark.
- The Seal-Warped Beast encounter includes mutation options.
- The Bell-Eater Grub has a usable D&D 5e boss profile.
- The archive escape has a 5-success-before-3-failures skill challenge.
- The final Act I map points to the three Act II destinations and the hidden fourth mark beneath the temple.
- Every major NPC has a combat role, roleplay function, secret, and Foundry Actor target.
